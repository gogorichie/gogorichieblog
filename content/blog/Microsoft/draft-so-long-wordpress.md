In early 2019, I took down my WordPress site and replaced it with a static HTML5 landing page while I explored alternatives to managing a WordPress site without investing excessive time. Eventually, I settled on Hugo.

**Love and Frustration with WordPress**

Over the years, my relationship with WordPress was characterized by both admiration and frustration. The platform's flexibility, allowing customization through a plethora of plugins, initially drew me in. I appreciated the freedom to tailor the platform to my needs, posting nearly 3000 blog entries over time. However, this very flexibility became a double-edged sword.

My WordPress journey began on livejournal.com, migrated to blogspot.com, and eventually became a self-hosted site on GoDaddy in 2011. While WordPress provided the freedom to shape content, design, and features, the trade-off was the constant need to manage databases, plugins, and CMS software. Neglecting these aspects posed a security risk, as a compromised site could be used to disseminate harmful content.

The management of a WordPress site often felt like a full-time job, even without creating content. While there are tools to simplify administration, the average blogger, seeking only to share knowledge or content, may find this daunting. My experience reached a tipping point where I spent more time patching and updating plugins, WordPress, and the backend MySQL server than creating meaningful content. The past three years witnessed significant attacks on self-hosted WordPress sites, adding to the challenges of maintaining a secure and functional platform.

**Where Am I Now**

So, I set out for more common waters and started running my blog with Hugo, GitHub, and Azure Static Web, bringing a seamless and efficient approach to my simple content. Hugo, a simple static content management system, gives me the flexibility to focus on the content I want to share and less on the backend needs. Once I'm finished with a post, I'm able to commit to a GitHub repo. For actual hosting, I'm using Azure Static Web, which is free for my needs and offers all the services I need with an automated deployment pipeline. Changes made on GitHub trigger a smooth update on the Azure platform, ensuring that the blog is always up-to-date and easily accessible. This trifecta of Hugo, GitHub, and Azure Static Web empowers me to share my thoughts and content with the world.

In embracing the simplicity and efficiency of Hugo, GitHub, and Azure Static Web, I've found a renewed passion for sharing my thoughts and content with the world. This trio has liberated me from the complexities of managing a WordPress site, allowing me to focus on what truly matters—creating meaningful content. As I continue this journey in more familiar waters, the seamless integration of these tools has not only streamlined my blogging experience but also ensured a secure and up-to-date platform. With each post committed to GitHub, and every update smoothly deployed on Azure Static Web, I am empowered to navigate the ever-evolving landscape of online content creation. Here's to a future filled with more sharing, learning, and connecting.
